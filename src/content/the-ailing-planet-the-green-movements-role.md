# The Ailing Planet: The Green Movement's Role

📝 **Short Summary:** Written in 1994, this article highlights the declining health of Earth. It discusses overpopulation, the depletion of the four biological systems (fisheries, forests, grasslands, croplands), and the need for humanity to act as responsible stewards.

1. **Simple Explanation:** Earth is compared to a patient in declining health. Humans are the most dangerous animals because we consume resources recklessly. We must shift from a mechanistic view to a holistic and ecological view of the world.
2. **Important Points:** 
   - Mr. Lester R. Brown's four principal biological systems.
   - The concept of "Sustainable Development" (meeting present needs without compromising the future).
3. **Exam Questions:** *Why is the earth said to be an 'ailing planet'? (6 Marks)*
4. **Answer Writing Tips:** Treat this like an essay. Use quotes from the chapter: *"We have not inherited this earth from our forefathers; we have borrowed it from our children."* (Lester Brown).
