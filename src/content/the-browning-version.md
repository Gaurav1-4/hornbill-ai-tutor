# The Browning Version

📝 **Short Summary:** A play excerpt where a student, Taplow, is waiting for his strict teacher, Mr. Crocker-Harris. Another teacher, Frank, tries to make Taplow mock Crocker-Harris, but Taplow reveals he actually respects and likes the strict teacher.

1. **Simple Explanation:** Taplow is doing extra work for Mr. Crocker-Harris. Frank, a young science teacher, is jealous of the respect Crocker-Harris commands, even though Crocker-Harris is emotionally completely closed off ("shriveled up inside like a nut").
2. **Important Points:** 
   - Taplow's mixed feelings: he fears Crocker-Harris but likes him.
   - Crocker-Harris's wife, Millie, sends Taplow to the chemist to free him.
3. **Character Analysis:** Mr. Crocker-Harris is rigid, rule-bound, but fair. Frank is young, envious, and unprofessional. Taplow is an observant and genuine student.
4. **Exam Questions:** *What is Taplow's attitude towards Mr. Crocker-Harris? (6 Marks)*
5. **Answer Writing Tips:** Highlight the phrase *“he is not a sadist”* to show Taplow's defense of his teacher.
