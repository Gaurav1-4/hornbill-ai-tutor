# Discovering Tut: The Saga Continues

📝 **Short Summary:** This chapter details the discovery of King Tutankhamun's tomb by Howard Carter in 1922 and the modern CT scan done in 2005 to unravel the mysteries of his young life and sudden death.

1. **Simple Explanation:** King Tut was the last heir of a powerful Egyptian dynasty. Because his tomb was filled with gold, he became famous. Carter's excavation damaged the mummy, but Zahi Hawass used a CT scan to get accurate medical data, revealing that he likely didn't die a natural death.
2. **Important Points:** 
   - The curse of the Pharaoh (winds blowing when the mummy was moved).
   - Amenhotep IV's (Akhenaten) religious revolution (promoting Aten).
3. **Character Analysis:** King Tut is the boy king who restored the old ways. Howard Carter is the dedicated but slightly destructive 1920s archaeologist.
4. **Exam Questions:** *Why did Howard Carter have to chisel away the solid resins? (3 Marks)*
5. **Answer Writing Tips:** Clearly distinguish between Carter's primitive 1922 methods and the advanced 2005 CT scan. Keywords: *computed tomography*, *resin*, *Akhenaten*.
