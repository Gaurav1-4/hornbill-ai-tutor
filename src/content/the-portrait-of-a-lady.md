# The Portrait of a Lady

📝 **Short Summary:** The author traces his relationship with his grandmother through three phases: childhood in the village, adolescence in the city, and his departure for higher studies. As time passes, they grow apart, but her love and religious devotion remain constant until her death.

1. **Simple Explanation:** The story is a loving tribute to the author's grandmother. In the village, they were inseparable. Moving to the city created a gap because she couldn't help him with English education and disliked music. She accepted her seclusion silently. 
2. **Important Points:** 
   - The sparrows mourning her death without eating breadcrumbs shows her pure soul.
   - The spinning wheel and prayers became her only refuge.
3. **Character Analysis:** The Grandmother is depicted as a strong, highly religious, and kind-hearted woman. Though old and wrinkled, she possessed a spiritual beauty (compared to the "winter landscape in the mountains").
4. **Exam Questions:** *Why was the grandmother unhappy with the city education? (3 Marks)*
5. **Answer Writing Tips:** Always highlight her transition from a highly involved guardian in the village to a silent, prayerful observer in the city. Use keywords like *spiritual beauty*, *resignation*, and *silent mourning*.
